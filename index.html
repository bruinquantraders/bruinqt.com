About Us

A club that builds a reputation, not just holds events.

Bruin Quant Traders is a focused group of UCLA undergraduate and graduate students serious about quantitative finance. We pair theory with applied coding, turn real projects into teaching material, and track where our members land — because placement is the strongest signal a quant club can send.

📈

Education

A hands-on curriculum: Green Book sessions, coding workshops, and reading groups that turn concepts into resume-ready skills.

⚙️

Projects

Quarter-length research and client projects producing real deliverables — Python bots, pricers, and backtested strategies.

🏆

Competition

A flagship, sponsor-backed trading competition and a demo day where project groups present strategies with citable PnLs.

🤝

Industry

Direct firm relationships, info sessions, and an alumni network that turn into recruiting pipelines for our members.

Education

Theory paired with applied coding.

We don't do passive lectures. Members learn what an order book is — and then code one. Every session is built to be practical, industry-relevant, and engaging.

01

Portfolio Theory

Optimization, risk models, and allocation — from the fundamentals to applied implementation.

02

Market Microstructure

Order books, execution, and market-making mechanics, taught through building a limit order book.

03

Machine Learning in Finance

Signals, forecasting, and modeling applied to real market data and live projects.

04

Math & Statistical Foundations

The probability, stochastic, and statistical backbone behind every strategy we build.

Research Projects

Members ship real, citable work.

Project groups pick a strategy or research direction, get paper-trading accounts, and build on top of a club-wide data repository and backtesting engine. The output is proof — not just slides.

Volatility

Multi-Horizon Volatility Estimator

A volatility estimation framework across multiple time horizons, turned into teaching material for the education track.

C++ / Systems

Limit Order Book Simulator

A C++ limit order book simulator that takes members from "what is an order book" to building one from scratch.

ML / Signals

Wavelet-Based Volatility Forecasting

Signal-processing meets forecasting — a research project that anchors our machine-learning reading group.

Infrastructure

Data Repository & Backtesting Engine

Shared club infrastructure so every strategy has clean data and a reproducible backtest before it ever goes live.

The Competition

The Bruin Trading Competition

Our flagship spring event: a sponsor-backed, intercollegiate trading competition. Groups develop a strategy through the year, then implement, test, and improve it with results tracked live — capped by a demo day of real PnLs in front of firm judges.
